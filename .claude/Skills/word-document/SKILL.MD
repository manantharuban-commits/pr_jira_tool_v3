# Style Guide: Professional & Rich Word Documents

Creating a document that looks polished and executive requires consistency. Follow these core rules for typography, color, spacing, and layout to elevate your Word documents.

## 1. Typography (Fonts)
Limit your document to a maximum of **two font families** (one for headings, one for body text).

*   **Body Text:** Use highly legible serif or sans-serif fonts. 
    *   *Examples:* Calibri (11 pt), Arial (10-11 pt), Aptos (11 pt), or Times New Roman (11-12 pt).
*   **Headings:** Use a font that contrasts but complements the body.
    *   *Examples:* Aptos Display, Arial Black, Segoe UI, or Cambria.
*   **Hierarchy:**
    *   **Title:** 24–28 pt, Bold (Primary color)
    *   **Heading 1:** 16–18 pt, Bold (Primary color)
    *   **Heading 2:** 13–14 pt, Bold (Secondary color)
    *   **Heading 3:** 11–12 pt, Bold (Dark Neutral/Black)

## 2. Color Palette
Establish a professional 3-to-4 color palette and stick to it strictly. Avoid overly bright or neon colors.

*   **Primary Color (60%):** Used for titles, Heading 1s, and major accents. (e.g., Deep Navy, Slate Blue, or Forest Green).
*   **Secondary Color (30%):** Used for Heading 2s, subtle accents, or data callouts. (e.g., Lighter blues, steel gray).
*   **Neutral Dark (10%):** Used for body text to ensure high readability. (e.g., Charcoal or Dark Gray, rather than pure black).
*   **Neutral Light:** Used for background shading, tables, and callout boxes. (e.g., Light Gray, Soft Ivory).

## 3. Layout and Spacing
Proper whitespace prevents documents from looking cluttered and improves readability.

*   **Margins:** Set to **1 inch (2.54 cm)** on all sides. Use "Mirrored" margins if you are printing double-sided.
*   **Line Spacing:** Set body text to **1.15 to 1.25** to give text "breathing room."
*   **Paragraph Spacing:** Add **6 pt to 8 pt after paragraphs** instead of pressing Enter twice.
*   **Alignment:** 
    *   **Body Text:** Left-align (ragged right) for the best readability. 
    *   **Headings:** Left-align for clean structure.
*   **Pagination:** Use *Page Breaks* (Ctrl + Enter) instead of repeatedly pressing Enter to start a new page. Keep paragraphs together with "Widow/Orphan control."

## 4. Visual Components & Data
Enhance the visual appeal of text-heavy documents using structured components.

*   **Cover Page:** Always use a clean, minimalist cover page for reports, proposals, or long documents. Center the title, subtitle, and date, and include a thin horizontal line for separation.
*   **Tables:** Avoid default Word table styles. Apply a custom style: primary colored header with white text, alternating row shading (zebra striping) in light gray, and left-aligned text (except numbers, which should be right-aligned).
*   **Callout Boxes:** Use shaded boxes with a solid primary-colored left border to highlight important notes, warnings, or quotes.
*   **Icons:** Use simple, flat, monochromatic icons to break up bulleted lists or highlight specific sections.
*   **Images:** Wrap text tightly around images ("Square" or "Tight" wrapping). Do not stretch images; hold `Shift` while resizing to maintain the aspect ratio.

## 5. Navigation and Structure
A rich document is easy to skim and navigate.

*   **Navigation Pane:** Always utilize Word's built-in *Styles* pane for headings. This automatically generates a functional Document Map/Navigation Pane.
*   **Headers & Footers:** Include subtle, muted-gray headers and footers. The header can include the document title, while the footer should contain the page number (e.g., Page 1 of 10).
*   **Table of Contents:** Include a dynamic Table of Contents on page 2 for any document longer than 5 pages.
*   **List Formatting:** Use exact, uniform bullets or numbered lists. Do not mix bullet styles within the same list.

## 6. Document Finishing Touches
Before exporting or sharing, ensure the document is pristine.

*   **Gridlines:** Temporarily turn on gridlines (View > Gridlines) when manually aligning shapes or images.
*   **Spell Check:** Run the built-in Editor (F7) to eliminate typos.
*   **PDF Export:** Never share `.docx` files externally unless collaboration is required. Export as a **PDF** (`.pdf`) to preserve your exact fonts, formatting, and layout across all devices.
